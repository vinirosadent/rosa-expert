A/P Rosa appointed to Singapore's National Mirror Working Group for ISO/TC 194

The committee represents Singapore's interests in the development and review of international standards for the biological and clinical evaluation of medical devices.

A/P Vinicius Rosa has been appointed as a Member of the National Mirror Working Group (NMWG) for ISO/TC 194 — Biological and Clinical Evaluation of Medical Devices. The NMWG plays a critical role in representing Singapore's interests in the development and review of international standards, aligning national positions with the work of ISO technical committees.

ISO/TC 194 develops the standards that govern how the biological safety of medical and dental devices is assessed before they reach patients — from biocompatibility testing through to clinical evaluation frameworks. Its work underpins patient safety, regulatory science and the translation of new biomaterials into clinical practice.

ISO/TC 194 is a cornerstone of patient safety, regulatory science and translational innovation.

A/P Rosa's appointment brings an academic and translational research perspective into Singapore's contribution to the international standards ecosystem, connecting frontline biomaterials research with the regulatory frameworks that shape how medical devices are evaluated worldwide.
