A/P Rosa co-authors international guidance on biocompatibility and pulp regeneration

A new guidance document co-authored by A/P Rosa addresses the standards and methods for evaluating biocompatibility in vital pulp therapy and regenerative endodontics — updating a decade of research into actionable criteria for the field.

A/P Vinicius Rosa joined an international group of researchers in authoring a guidance paper on the evaluation of biocompatibility for materials used in vital pulp therapy and regenerative endodontic procedures. Published in late 2024, the document addresses a longstanding gap in the field: while numerous materials have been proposed for direct and indirect pulp capping and regenerative endodontics, the criteria for assessing their biocompatibility have remained inconsistent across laboratories and regulatory frameworks.

The guidance builds on the foundation laid by an earlier landmark paper co-authored by A/P Rosa, which established a framework for assessing the biological properties of dental materials in the context of pulp biology. The 2024 document updates that framework in light of a decade of subsequent research, incorporating current in vitro models of dental pulp stem cells, refined in vivo designs, and improved assay systems that better reflect clinical conditions and the biological complexity of the dental pulp.

Guidance documents carry significant weight in the dental research community: they shape laboratory practice, inform regulatory submissions, and define the evidentiary standards against which new materials are evaluated. Co-authorship reflects A/P Rosa’s standing as one of the international authorities on dental pulp biocompatibility and his active role in shaping the methodological standards of the field. The document was developed by a multidisciplinary panel with representation from leading research groups across Europe, North America, and Asia.

About biocompatibility testing

Biocompatibility testing in dentistry evaluates whether a material is safe and appropriate for contact with oral tissues. It involves standardised in vitro assays (cell viability, genotoxicity) and in vivo animal models assessing pulp tissue response. International standards (ISO 10993) provide the general framework, but dental-specific guidance is essential for translating these into clinically relevant testing paradigms.
