A/P Rosa elected Fellow of the Academy of Dental Materials

The honour places A/P Rosa among the global leaders in dental materials science, as recognised by a peer-nominated fellowship within one of the field's most prestigious professional bodies.

The Academy of Dental Materials (ADM) has elected A/P Vinicius Rosa as a Fellow — one of its highest honours. The ADM Fellowship is awarded to active members who have made outstanding contributions to dental materials research, education, or service to the field. Election requires nomination and endorsement by existing Fellows, making it a direct expression of peer recognition at the international level.

A/P Rosa's research programme — spanning hydrogels for dental pulp tissue regeneration, bioactive cements, and predictive modelling of biomaterial performance — represents the kind of sustained, high-impact science that ADM Fellowship seeks to recognise. His contributions extend from fundamental materials characterisation to international guidelines for biocompatibility testing, placing his group among the most influential in the field globally.

Founded in 1959, the ADM is the professional home of scientists and clinicians dedicated to advancing dental materials research and education. It publishes Dental Materials — one of the most important scientific journals in the field — and supports professional development through meetings, awards, and educational initiatives. ADM Fellows span more than 40 countries and include many of the most distinguished researchers in dental science.

About the ADM

The Academy of Dental Materials was founded in 1959 and is the premier international professional organisation for dental materials scientists. Fellowship in the ADM is awarded by peer nomination and is considered one of the highest honours in the field. Members span over 40 countries and include leading researchers, educators, and clinicians.
