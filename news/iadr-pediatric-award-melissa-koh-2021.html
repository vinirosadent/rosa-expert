Melissa Koh Receives the IADR Pediatric Oral Health Research Group Student Award

Recognised for excellence in student research on the oral health of children and adolescents, at the IADR scientific meeting.

Melissa Koh received the IADR Pediatric Oral Health Research Group Student Award in 2021, with the project supervised within the pediatric oral health research community.

The IADR Pediatric Oral Health Research Group Student Award recognizes excellence in research focused on the oral health of children and adolescents. The award is intended for dental students, postgraduate students, and residents in specialty training programs, and is judged on the quality of the research, presentation, and responses to questions during the IADR scientific meeting.

“

Research in this field contributes to better understanding of childhood oral diseases, risk factors, prevention strategies, and treatment approaches.

Melissa’s recognition is significant because pediatric oral health research sits at the intersection of prevention, biology, clinical care, and public health. Research in this field contributes to better understanding of childhood oral diseases, risk factors, prevention strategies, and treatment approaches that can improve oral health outcomes early in life.

Receiving this award places Melissa’s work among highly regarded student contributions to pediatric oral health research and reflects the value of developing strong research training within postgraduate dental education. It also underscores the importance of mentorship in supporting young researchers as they translate clinical questions into rigorous scientific work.
