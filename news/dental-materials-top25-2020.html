Two papers by A/P Rosa rank among the 25 most cited in Dental Materials

Research from the group on bioactive materials and dental pulp biology appears among the most influential papers published in one of the field's leading journals.

The journal Dental Materials — the flagship publication of the Academy of Dental Materials — identified its 25 most-cited recent articles, and two papers from A/P Rosa's research programme were included. Highly cited publications are a meaningful indicator of scientific influence: they mark work that other researchers have found important enough to reference and build upon in their own studies. For a mid-career researcher, placing two papers in such a list signals exceptional reach and impact.

The two papers represent core threads of A/P Rosa's scientific programme: one addressing the design and characterisation of bioactive calcium silicate cements capable of promoting dentinogenic mineralisation, and the other exploring how dental pulp cells respond to biomaterial stimuli at the molecular level. Both papers reflect an approach that integrates materials engineering with cell biology — a combination that has defined the group's contributions to the international dental materials community.

It ranks among the most important peer-reviewed journals in dental science, covering topics from dental polymers and ceramics to adhesives, bioactive cements, and tissue-engineering scaffolds. Placement among its most-cited articles signals sustained impact on the work of the broader international research community.

About Dental Materials

Dental Materials is the flagship journal of the Academy of Dental Materials, published by Elsevier. It is one of the most widely read and cited journals in dental biomaterials science, covering polymers, ceramics, composites, adhesives, bioactive cements, and tissue engineering. Its most-cited articles reflect the directions that have most shaped the field.
