Klara Koh Receives the IADR-SEA Hatton Award Junior Category

One of the most prestigious recognitions for student researchers in dental, oral, and craniofacial sciences, awarded within the Southeast Asian Division of the IADR.

Klara Koh, undergraduate student supervised by A/P Vinicius Rosa, received the IADR-SEA Hatton Award Junior Category in 2018, one of the most prestigious recognitions for student researchers in dental, oral, and craniofacial sciences.

The Hatton competition has long been regarded as a major platform for emerging investigators, giving outstanding dental students and early-career researchers the opportunity to present their work to the wider research community. Within the Southeast Asian Division, the award identifies promising young researchers whose projects demonstrate scientific quality, clarity of presentation, and relevance to oral health.

“

Awards such as the IADR-SEA Hatton Junior Category help launch young investigators into the international research environment.

Klara’s recognition reflects the strength of student-led research and the importance of early exposure to scientific inquiry during dental training. Awards such as the IADR-SEA Hatton Junior Category do more than celebrate an individual achievement; they help launch young investigators into the international research environment and encourage the development of the next generation of clinician-scientists.

This achievement also highlights the role of mentorship and research culture in creating opportunities for students to engage with meaningful scientific questions from the beginning of their professional careers.
